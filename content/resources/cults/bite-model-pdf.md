The BITE Model represents the most widely used and validated framework for evaluating whether any group exhibits cult-like characteristics and functions as a high-control group. Developed by cult exit counselor Steven Hassan, the model identifies four domains of control: Behavior Control, Information Control, Thought Control, and Emotional Control. The freely available PDF checklist allows anyone to evaluate groups on a spectrum of control rather than relying on categorical definitions.

The model's power lies in its universality—it applies equally to religious cults, MLMs, corporate environments, and online communities. By focusing on control mechanisms rather than surface ideology, the BITE Model reveals that high-control dynamics operate similarly across vastly different contexts. This framework has become essential for both research and practical evaluation, helping people recognize exploitative group dynamics they might not have recognized under traditional cult definitions.

For those concerned about their involvement in a group, a friend's involvement, or trying to understand their past experiences, the BITE Model provides an objective assessment tool that can clarify whether cult-like control is occurring. The framework also helps people distinguish between healthy authority, organizational hierarchy, and exploitative control. Having a concrete, non-judgmental tool for evaluation can be profoundly validating for those questioning their group involvement.

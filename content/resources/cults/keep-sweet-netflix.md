Keep Sweet: Pray and Obey is a Netflix documentary that provides comprehensive coverage of the Fundamentalist Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (FLDS) and its leader Warren Jeffs. The series combines archival footage, survivor interviews, and expert commentary to document the group's history, theological framework, practices of forced plural marriage and child abuse, and the eventual legal prosecution of Jeffs.

The documentary is particularly valuable for its extensive survivor testimony, allowing FLDS members and former members to explain what they experienced and how they understood their lives within the group. The series traces Jeffs's rise to power, the escalation of authoritarian control, the sexual exploitation of minors, and the resistance that eventually led to law enforcement intervention. The materials demonstrate how theological claims about religious freedom were weaponized to protect abuse.

For those deconstructing from FLDS or fundamentalist Christianity more broadly, Keep Sweet provides both validation and educational resources for family members seeking to understand what happened to their loved ones. The documentary demonstrates that FLDS practices, while presented as religious tradition, constituted systematic abuse and trafficking. It shows that communities can resist high-control groups and that legal accountability is possible.

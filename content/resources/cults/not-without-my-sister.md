Not Without My Sister is a powerful collaborative memoir by three sisters—Kristina, Celeste, and Juliana—who were raised in the Children of God (later called The Family International) cult. The book documents their childhood within the isolated cult community, exposure to sexual abuse and exploitation, and their eventual escape. The sisters' narrative reveals the systemic abuse of children within the organization, the theological justifications offered for that abuse, and the psychological impact of growing up in such an environment.

The memoir is distinctive for its multi-perspective approach, allowing readers to understand how the same experiences affected three different individuals across their lifespans. The sisters document both the immediate trauma of abuse and the long-term impacts on identity development, trust, and relationships. Importantly, they emphasize that their mother's involvement in the group was not a personal failure but reflected how cults deliberately exploit and manipulate even well-intentioned parents.

For those raised in cults or high-control groups, Not Without My Sister provides both validation and hope. The sisters' journey from victimization to agency and recovery demonstrates that healing is possible. The book also provides essential resources for understanding how cult environments uniquely damage children and how recovery must address developmental trauma. For parents concerned about cult involvement or evaluating their own past experiences, the memoir offers perspective on cult impact across generations.

Seduced is a Starz documentary series that tells the NXIVM story from the perspective of India Oxenberg, the daughter of actor Catherine Oxenberg, who was recruited into NXIVM's innermost circle and subjected to the organization's most intense exploitation and control. The series documents how Oxenberg gradually recognized NXIVM's coercive tactics, the trauma of her experiences, and her process of leaving and recovery.

What distinguishes Seduced from other NXIVM documentaries is its intimate focus on one member's journey through recruitment, deepening involvement, recognition of abuse, and exit. Viewers witness the psychological sophistication of NXIVM's recruitment and manipulation, including how Oxenberg's privilege and education did not protect her from sophisticated psychological control. The documentary emphasizes how high-control groups deliberately target intelligent, accomplished people and deploy advanced psychological manipulation.

For those in recovery from NXIVM or similar groups, seeing Oxenberg's journey—including her anger, grief, and eventual healing—can be profoundly validating. The documentary demonstrates that recovery from cult involvement is possible and that survivors can reclaim their lives and agency. For family members of people in cults, the series shows how families can support members in recognizing exploitation and facilitating exit.

The Jonestown Institute, maintained by San Diego State University, is the definitive comprehensive archive on the Peoples Temple and the Jonestown tragedy of 1978 in which 918 people died. The institute houses thousands of declassified FBI documents (public domain), primary source materials, survivor testimonies, academic analyses, and photographs that provide unprecedented access to understanding how a high-control group evolved and the conditions that led to mass tragedy.

The archive's strength lies in its combination of primary sources and contextualization. Visitors can read FBI documents, watch video testimonies from survivors, access demographic data about Peoples Temple membership, study financial records revealing the group's operations, and read academic articles examining cult dynamics, psychological factors, and social contexts. The materials demonstrate how charismatic leadership, theological manipulation, isolation, and increasing authoritarianism created conditions for tragedy.

For those studying cults, understanding cult dynamics, or processing their own group experiences, the Jonestown Institute provides both cautionary lessons and profound validation. The archive demonstrates that cult tragedies don't emerge from isolated individual pathology but from structural group dynamics that can develop in any high-control group. Survivor accounts in particular offer hope that people do escape, survive, and recover, even from the most controlled environments.

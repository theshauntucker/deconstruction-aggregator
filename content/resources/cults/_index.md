Cults and high-control groups represent organizational structures that exploit and control members through psychological manipulation, isolation, and authoritarian leadership. These groups transcend any particular religious tradition or ideology; rather, they are characterized by specific structural features: concentrated power in leadership, information control, thought control, and emotional manipulation. Cult-like dynamics emerge in religious organizations (Christian, Buddhist, Hindu), secular movements (NXIVM, Twin Flames Universe), conspiracy communities (QAnon), and wellness spaces.

The BITE Model—developed by cult researcher Steven Hassan and standing for Behavior, Information, Thought, and Emotional control—provides a framework for evaluating whether any group functions as a high-control group on a spectrum of control. This model reveals that cult dynamics operate similarly across different surface ideologies, from apocalyptic religious groups to modern tech companies with cult-like cultures.

This section provides resources for understanding cult dynamics, evaluating groups for cult-like characteristics, accessing support during and after involvement, and learning from historical and contemporary cases. Whether someone is currently in a high-control group, recently left one, or is concerned about a loved one's involvement, these materials provide education, validation, and pathways to recovery.

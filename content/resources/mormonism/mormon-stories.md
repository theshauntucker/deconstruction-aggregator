Mormon Stories is the flagship podcast of the ex-Mormon community. Launched in 2005 by John Dehlin, it has grown to over 1,700 episodes and has become the most influential audio resource for people navigating Mormon faith transitions. Dehlin himself was excommunicated in 2015 for his work on the podcast, which only amplified its credibility within the community.

What makes Mormon Stories exceptional is the depth and breadth of its interviews. Rather than quick soundbites, Dehlin conducts multi-hour conversations that allow guests to tell their complete stories and explore complex topics thoroughly. Episodes feature former Church leaders, scholars, historians, LGBTQ Mormons navigating faith, families torn apart by faith differences, and people at every stage of deconstruction. The podcast treats subjects with intellectual seriousness while maintaining genuine human connection.

The podcast's reach extends beyond people actively deconstructing. Believing Mormons sometimes listen to understand why people leave. Scholars and historians use it as a platform to discuss research. The podcast has essentially become a public archive of Mormon deconstruction in audio form, with searchable archives and transcripts.

Dehlin's personal commitment to the work—continuing the podcast even after his excommunication, without major financial support for years—demonstrates why the community trusts him. He hasn't profited from the podcast or monetized it heavily. He's simply provided a platform for voices that the Church suppresses. For many people, Mormon Stories represents their first exposure to the possibility that leaving faith isn't shameful or wrong—it's a valid human experience shared by thousands.

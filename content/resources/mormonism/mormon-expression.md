Mormon Expression represents an important historical moment in ex-Mormon media: the era before the current explosion of podcasts, when Mormon Expression was one of the few long-form audio resources for people questioning the faith. Hosted by John Larsen, the podcast ran from 2009 to 2015, producing hundreds of episodes covering a wide range of topics in Mormon history, theology, and culture with analytical depth.

Although the podcast is no longer producing new episodes, the archive remains valuable. Larsen's approach combined scholarly research, personal reflection, and intellectual rigor. Episodes diving into specific topics—the Book of Abraham, temple ceremonies, Joseph Smith's polygamy, First Vision variations—provided thorough coverage that helped listeners understand both what the problems were and why they mattered. The podcast demonstrated that serious intellectual engagement with Mormon deconstruction was possible before the current ecosystem of resources existed.

Mormon Expression was a pioneer. It helped establish that the ex-Mormon community had serious scholars and researchers capable of producing substantive content. The podcast's existence, long before the mainstream attention that later projects received, indicated that faith transitions and Mormon criticism were significant enough topics to sustain ongoing analytical discourse.

For people interested in the history of ex-Mormon media or those searching for in-depth analysis of specific historical topics, Mormon Expression's archives remain accessible. The podcast serves as a document of how people were thinking about and discussing Mormon issues in the early 2010s—often with sophisticated analysis that predicted later scholarly developments. While newer podcasts may be more accessible, Mormon Expression remains valuable for anyone seeking comprehensive historical analysis.

The Hulu series adaptation of "Under the Banner of Heaven" brought mainstream attention to issues within Mormon history that had previously received limited general audience exposure. The series dramatizes Jon Krakauer's investigation into the 1984 murders committed by fundamentalist extremists, interweaving this crime story with historical flashbacks showing how foundational Mormon theology connects to modern fundamentalist violence.

The show's power lies in its accessibility. Most Americans have never encountered the concept of blood atonement or understood how Mormon theological traditions could lead to extreme violence. By dramatizing these connections through a compelling narrative featuring Andrew Garfield as a detective slowly uncovering these truths, the series makes available to mainstream audiences knowledge that previously required reading academic books or seeking out specialized resources.

The series does not present fundamentalism as completely separate from mainstream Mormonism. The narrative structure moves between present-day crime investigation and historical scenes showing Joseph Smith, Brigham Young, and early Mormon leadership explicitly teaching principles of blood atonement and violent retribution. This dramatization, while fictionalized in its specific details, conveys an important historical truth: fundamentalist violence did not emerge randomly but grew from actual historical Mormon teachings.

For Mormon deconstruction, the Hulu series served an important function in 2022: it introduced millions of viewers to the uncomfortable historical realities that people inside Mormonism had been grappling with for years. Families gathered to watch the series and then had conversations about what they had seen. The series functioned as a secular introduction to religious history that the institutional Church had minimized or denied. For some viewers, it prompted them to ask questions they had previously been taught not to ask.

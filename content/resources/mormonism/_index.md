## Overview

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS or Mormon Church) was founded by Joseph Smith in 1830 and claims approximately 16 million members worldwide. However, activity rates suggest the number of regular participants is substantially lower. The LDS Church represents one of the most organized and intellectually engaged deconstruction communities online, with an estimated 1-3 million ex-Mormons in various stages of faith transition or post-faith life.

## Core Issues in Mormon Deconstruction

The most significant truth claims under scrutiny in Mormon deconstruction include:

**Book of Mormon Historicity**: Archaeological, linguistic, and DNA evidence contradicts claims that the Book of Mormon describes real pre-Columbian American civilizations. No credible archaeological evidence supports the existence of the peoples, places, or events described in the Book of Mormon.

**Book of Abraham**: The LDS Church's 2014 Gospel Topics Essay admits that Joseph Smith's translation of the Book of Abraham was not actually a translation of the Egyptian papyri he possessed. Egyptologists and scholars have known for decades that the papyri describe Egyptian funerary practices, not the life of Abraham.

**First Vision Accounts**: Joseph Smith gave at least four different accounts of his foundational religious experience (the First Vision) between 1832 and 1838, with significant contradictions regarding who appeared to him (Jesus vs. God and Jesus), what he was asking about, and other details.

**Polygamy**: Joseph Smith secretly married 30+ women, many in their teens, some while married to other men, and lied about it to Church members and his wife Emma for years. The Church initially denied this practice, then admitted it in 2014. Brigham Young and other early leaders practiced polygamy openly until federal pressure forced the Church to officially discontinue it in 1890.

**Race and the Priesthood Ban**: The LDS Church banned Black members from full participation (specifically from the priesthood and temple ceremonies) from 1849 until 1978. The Church presented the ban as a divine principle but later acknowledged it was a policy based on racism without scriptural foundation.

**Temple Ceremonies**: The LDS Church teaches that its temple ceremonies are sacred and essential for salvation, but these ceremonies have changed dramatically over time, particularly regarding women's roles and the verbatim teaching of oaths to keep specific rituals secret. Many people report that the actual temple experience differs significantly from expectations created by pre-temple preparation.

**Historical Revisionism**: The LDS Church has systematically downplayed, denied, or hidden inconvenient historical facts for decades—from Joseph Smith's polygamy to Brigham Young's involvement in the Mountain Meadows Massacre to financial corruption scandals. Many of these facts only became widely known through independent researchers, whistleblowers, and internet accessibility.

**LGBTQ Treatment**: The LDS Church teaches that same-sex attraction is acceptable but same-sex activity is sinful, and it has historically excluded LGBTQ members from full participation. The Church's 2015 policy excluding children of LGBTQ parents from baptism and ordination caused significant controversy and contributed to many faith transitions.

## The Ex-Mormon Community

The ex-Mormon/post-Mormon community is one of the largest and most organized faith deconstruction communities in existence. The r/exmormon subreddit has over 300,000 members. Major annual conferences bring together hundreds of ex-Mormon scholars, authors, activists, and community members. Numerous podcasts, YouTube channels, websites, and grassroots organizations serve this community.

What distinguishes the Mormon deconstruction community from many other religious deconstruction spaces is the combination of:
- High intellectual engagement with historical and theological issues
- Strong emphasis on community support and social connection after leaving
- Substantial representation of second-generation members raised in the Church
- Significant family conflict because the Church teaches that leaving compromises salvation
- Access to unusually detailed primary source documentation and scholarly attention
- A culture of humor and irreverence that helps process complex emotions

## How to Use These Resources

Resources in this section range from beginner-friendly entry points to advanced academic scholarship. The **CES Letter** and **Letter for My Wife** are often the first documents people encounter. Subreddits like **r/exmormon** provide community and peer support. Podcasts like **Mormon Stories** offer in-depth interviews. Books like **No Man Knows My History** and **Rough Stone Rolling** provide comprehensive historical context.

Many people navigate Mormon deconstruction through stages: initial doubt, information gathering, community connection, and eventual faith transition. These resources can support any stage of that journey.

MormonThink.com is one of the oldest and most thoroughly researched online resources for investigating Mormonism. Launched in 2005, it functions as an encyclopedia of Mormon issues, with each topic receiving extended treatment from multiple viewpoints. The site explicitly aims to present both faithful LDS perspectives and critical scholarly perspectives side-by-side, allowing readers to evaluate arguments and evidence themselves.

What distinguishes MormonThink from purely critical sites is its commitment to representing the strongest possible versions of faithful arguments. For each issue—Book of Mormon historicity, Book of Abraham, First Vision, polygamy, etc.—the site presents the best LDS apologetic responses alongside the critical analysis. This transparency about what the other side actually argues is refreshing and makes the site valuable even for people still exploring faith rather than committed to leaving.

The site's organization makes it easy to navigate to specific topics of interest and dive deeply. Each topic includes a summary, an overview of the issue, faithful perspectives, critical perspectives, and often a discussion of why the issue matters. The writing is academic in tone but accessible to general readers. Graphics and timelines help visualize complex historical information.

MormonThink represents the work of multiple contributors over years, and while it's not perfect, it remains one of the most comprehensive single sources for understanding the major issues in Mormon faith transitions. Many people use it as a reference point when they encounter specific truth claims and want to understand what critics and apologists actually say about them. Its longevity (running for almost 20 years) speaks to its usefulness within the community.

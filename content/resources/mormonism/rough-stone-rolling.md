Richard Bushman's "Rough Stone Rolling" is paradoxically one of the most important books for Mormon deconstruction despite being written by a faithful LDS historian attempting to defend Smith. Bushman, a respected scholar and practicing Mormon, wrote what many consider the finest scholarly biography of Joseph Smith from a believing perspective. The book is meticulously researched, honestly presents historical evidence, and acknowledges problems that earlier Mormon biographies ignored or minimized.

Bushman acknowledges Joseph Smith's involvement in treasure hunting and folk magic before his prophetic career, the serious problems with the Book of Abraham translation, the existence of multiple contradictory First Vision accounts, Smith's deception about polygamy, and numerous other issues. Yet Bushman attempts to frame all of this within a narrative where Smith is still a genuine prophet—explaining away the problems through psychological complexity, cultural context, or mysterious divine processes beyond human understanding.

What makes the book paradoxically powerful for deconstruction is precisely this contrast: Bushman presents the historical evidence so clearly and thoroughly that many readers find his faithful interpretations unconvincing. If Smith was genuinely deceived or lying about polygamy, how can we trust his other revelations? If the Book of Abraham translation is problematic, what does that say about his prophetic authority? Bushman's book essentially documents all the reasons one might doubt Smith while asking readers to maintain faith anyway—which many people find impossible once the full historical picture becomes clear.

Many ex-Mormons report that reading "Rough Stone Rolling" was the turning point in their deconstruction. Bushman's scholarship is rigorous enough that it cannot be dismissed, yet the historical facts it presents are damaging enough to Smith's claims that staying within faith becomes psychologically difficult. The book demonstrates that one can take Mormon history seriously, engage with complete historical evidence, and still find oneself unable to maintain belief in Smith's prophetic claims.

General and cross-tradition resources applicable to deconstruction from any faith tradition. This section includes mental health and crisis support resources, materials addressing conspiracy and cult-like movements that transcend specific religions, and tools for evaluating groups and dynamics regardless of their theological framing.

Many people deconstructing from religion experience serious mental health crises including depression, suicidality, identity loss, and trauma. Crisis hotlines and mental health resources specific to religious trauma and religious deconstruction are included here, along with materials that help validate experiences of religious harm and support healing. Additionally, this section covers how conspiracy movements and wellness culture exhibit cult-like dynamics, affecting people across religious backgrounds and secular worldviews alike.

Whether you're deconstructing from a specific tradition or processing the broader impacts of religious involvement, these general resources provide support, validation, and pathways to recovery applicable across contexts.

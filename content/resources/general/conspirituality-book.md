## Editorial Commentary

This groundbreaking investigation directly addresses the intersection of spiritual seeking and conspiracy culture that affects countless people in wellness communities. Ward, Kendal, and Remski provide rigorous documentation of how practices like energy work, crystal spirituality, and wellness ideology created psychological preconditions for conspiracy radicalization—a pattern that accelerated dramatically during the COVID-19 pandemic.

The book's particular value lies in its sympathetic treatment of individuals caught in these dynamics while maintaining critical analysis of the underlying mechanisms. Many former members of New Age communities report that their spiritual practices were explicitly weaponized by conspiracy promoters, with language about "awakening" and "seeing the truth" repurposed to draw them into QAnon narratives. This resource is essential for understanding how institutional spiritual instruction can become a radicalization pathway.

The authors examine specific case studies where established New Age practitioners became QAnon promoters, how alternative health communities became vectors for conspiracy spread, and why spiritual frameworks created vulnerability to conspiratorial thinking. This is critical reading for anyone deconstructing from wellness-oriented spiritual communities and attempting to understand how they became entangled with extremism.

Educated is Tara Westover's stunning memoir of growing up in an isolationist, survivalist Mormon family in rural Idaho where she received no formal education and was subjected to physical abuse, medical neglect, and emotional manipulation. Westover's narrative of teaching herself to read, eventually earning a high school equivalency, and then attending college and earning a PhD from Cambridge is a testament to human resilience and the power of education as liberation.

The memoir documents how religious fundamentalism, combined with survivalist ideology and family dysfunction, created an environment of control, fear, and abuse. Westover describes how her family's beliefs isolated them from mainstream society, how her parents used religion to justify refusing medical care, and how questioning these beliefs felt like betrayal. Her journey of gaining education and independent thought meant gradually distancing from her family, a painful but necessary process.

For those raised in fundamentalist or isolationist religious environments, Educated provides both validation and inspiration. Westover demonstrates that people can escape educational deprivation, recover from abuse, and build independent lives even after severe restrictions. Her story shows that religious harm is real and that leaving religion can be a path to freedom and flourishing. The memoir also provides crucial perspective for those concerned about relatives in isolated or controlling religious communities.

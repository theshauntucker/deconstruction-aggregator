Dr. Marlene Winell's peer-reviewed research on Religious Trauma Syndrome (RTS) has been groundbreaking in establishing that religious harm constitutes a form of trauma requiring specific therapeutic approaches. Her articles, published in the British Association for Behavioural and Cognitive Psychotherapies journal, provide scientific validation that the psychological damage from religious indoctrination and control is real, measurable, and distinct from general trauma.

Winell's work identifies the specific mechanisms through which religious indoctrination causes trauma, including fear-based teachings, shame cultivation, loss of autonomy, identity confusion, and rupture of primary relationships. Her research demonstrates that these harms occur across traditions and are not limited to extreme cases but emerge in many mainstream religious communities. The articles provide a framework for understanding religious trauma that therapists, survivors, and researchers can use for education and treatment.

For those deconstructing from religion and seeking validation that their psychological distress is legitimate, Winell's work provides crucial scientific backing. The articles demonstrate that religious trauma is not personal weakness or spiritual immaturity but a documented psychological outcome of high-control religious environments. Having peer-reviewed research validate your experience can be profoundly affirming and can help in accessing appropriate mental health treatment.

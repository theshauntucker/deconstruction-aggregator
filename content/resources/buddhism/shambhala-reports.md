Shambhala International publicly released official reports documenting sexual abuse and misconduct within the organization, primarily involving its leader Sakyong Mipham Rinpoche. These reports emerged after years of survivor activism and represent a significant moment of institutional acknowledgment, though critics argue the process stopped short of full accountability and meaningful reform.

The publicly available reports detail patterns of sexual abuse, manipulation of followers, and the use of spiritual authority to coerce sexual compliance. They document how organizational leaders were aware of misconduct but failed to protect members, instead prioritizing institutional reputation and the protection of the teacher. The reports reveal how Buddhist concepts like karma and acceptance were weaponized to silence survivors and discourage reporting.

While the existence of these public reports represents a step toward accountability, many survivors and advocates have noted that institutional change at Shambhala remains incomplete. The reports serve as important historical documentation of abuse dynamics within a major Buddhist organization and provide valuable insight into how harm becomes normalized and concealed in high-control spiritual environments.

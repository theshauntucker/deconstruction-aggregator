Behind the Bastards, the wildly popular podcast hosted by Robert Evans, has produced several episodes examining abusive Buddhist leaders and organizations, bringing Buddhist abuse dynamics to a mainstream audience. Evans combines historical research, dark humor, and genuine concern for survivors to make complex abuse patterns accessible to listeners unfamiliar with Buddhist institutions or deconstruction issues.

The podcast's coverage of figures like Sogyal Rinpoche and organizations like Shambhala reaches audiences who might not encounter this material through academic or survivor-oriented sources. Evans's approach—taking these issues seriously while refusing reverence for spiritual authority—models a healthy skepticism toward charismatic religious figures. The episodes provide clear explanations of how guru dynamics enable abuse and why survivors often struggle to be believed by their communities.

For those deconstructing from Buddhism, the podcast serves as both validation and orientation tool. Hearing an external voice take abuse allegations seriously can be powerfully affirming for people whose concerns were previously dismissed as disrespectful or spiritually immature. The podcast also serves an important educational function for friends and family members trying to understand what happened to their loved ones in Buddhist communities.

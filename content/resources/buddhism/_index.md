Buddhism is often perceived in the West as a peaceful, benign tradition centered on meditation and philosophical inquiry. However, institutional Buddhism has experienced significant abuse scandals that reveal the vulnerability of all religious traditions to hierarchical exploitation. Key issues include documented sexual abuse by senior teachers, the problematic dynamics of guru devotion that enable misconduct, spiritual bypassing that minimizes harm, cultural appropriation in Western contexts, and institutional cover-ups that prioritize reputation over survivor protection.

The "An Olive Branch" investigations stand as a watershed moment in Buddhist accountability, revealing systematic abuse across multiple organizations including Shambhala, Rigpa, Against the Stream, and numerous smaller centers. These investigations challenged the false narrative that Buddhist ethics and meditation practice create inherent safeguards against abuse. The findings demonstrate that institutional structures, teacher authority, and power imbalances in Buddhist communities function similarly to those in other high-control religious environments.

This section provides access to investigative reports, survivor accounts, academic analysis, and community resources for those deconstructing from Buddhism or processing harm caused within Buddhist institutions. Understanding these issues is essential for maintaining ethical engagement with Buddhist practice and supporting survivors.

An Olive Branch represents a watershed moment in Buddhist institutional accountability. This independent investigative organization conducted rigorous third-party investigations into sexual abuse, misconduct, and institutional cover-ups within major Buddhist organizations, particularly Shambhala and Against the Stream. The investigations are notable for their independence from the organizations under scrutiny and their commitment to survivor-centered methodology.

The publicly released reports from An Olive Branch provide detailed documentation of patterns of abuse, the organizational structures that enabled misconduct, and the various ways institutional leaders minimized survivor harm and protected accused teachers. These reports challenged the widespread assumption that Buddhist practice and philosophy create inherent protections against abuse, demonstrating instead that Buddhist organizations are subject to the same power dynamics and institutional failings as any other hierarchical group.

The accessibility and thoroughness of the An Olive Branch reports make them essential reading for anyone deconstructing from Buddhism or seeking to understand how abuse becomes normalized in spiritual communities. The reports are freely available online and serve as a model for how institutional accountability can function when carried out by independent investigators committed to survivor voices.

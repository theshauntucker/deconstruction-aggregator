The JW Child Abuse Database is a searchable repository of reported abuse cases within the JW organization, compiled from court records, victim reports, settlement data, and investigative journalism. The database allows researchers and affected individuals to see the scope and patterns of abuse across congregations and jurisdictions.

This resource transforms individual stories into quantifiable data, making the institutional scale of the abuse crisis impossible to ignore or minimize. The database reveals geographic patterns, the ages of victims, and relationships between abusers and their communities—information that demonstrates abuse is endemic rather than exceptional.

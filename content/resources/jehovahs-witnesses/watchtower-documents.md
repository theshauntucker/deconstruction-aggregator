This archive preserves internal Watchtower documents including legal correspondence, confidential letters to elders, internal memos on abuse cases, and organizational directives that were never intended for public scrutiny. These primary sources reveal how the organization handles crises internally versus how it presents itself publicly.

The archive is invaluable for researchers and journalists because it documents the gap between official public statements and internal institutional practice. Letters on child protection policies, abuse case handling, and disfellowshipping procedures show institutional decision-making unfiltered by public relations concerns.

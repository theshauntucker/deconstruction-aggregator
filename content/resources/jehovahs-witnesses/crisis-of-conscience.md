Raymond Franz served on the Watchtower's Governing Body—the small group of men who claim divine guidance and make all doctrinal decisions for 8+ million Witnesses—before being disfellowshipped in the 1980s. In *Crisis of Conscience*, he provides an unprecedented insider's view of how Watchtower leadership operates, including deliberate doctrinal flip-flops, suppression of dissent, and the profound disconnect between public teaching and private practice.

Franz's testimony is uniquely credible precisely because he was an architect of the system before rejecting it. His account documents how the Governing Body operates as a self-perpetuating authority structure, how doctrinal changes are rationalized and sometimes invented wholesale, and how organizational loyalty supersedes ethical considerations. For anyone attempting to understand JW institutional pathology, this remains the foundational firsthand testimony.

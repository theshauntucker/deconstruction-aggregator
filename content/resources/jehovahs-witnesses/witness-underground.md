Witness Underground is a unique documentary that examines the intersection of Jehovah's Witnesses and punk rock culture—documenting JWs who engaged in cultural rebellion while navigating strict religious control. The film explores how punks within the religion navigated their identity, music, and values against institutional demands.

The documentary provides cultural and sociological perspective on JW members' interior lives and resistance, showing that even within high-control religions, individuals find ways to assert agency and authenticity. It's a compelling entry point for understanding JW institutional control through a countercultural lens.

In this follow-up to *Crisis of Conscience*, Raymond Franz provides a rigorous theological and historical examination of core JW doctrinal positions, including their interpretation of scripture, claims about divine appointment, and the authority structure of the Governing Body. Rather than focusing on institutional politics, this book directly engages the theological claims that justify JW practices.

Franz methodically demonstrates how JW doctrine diverges from both mainstream Christianity and the historical context of early Christian writings. The book is essential for understanding how theological distortions serve institutional control—and how to recognize when doctrine has been shaped by organizational interests rather than consistent scriptural interpretation.

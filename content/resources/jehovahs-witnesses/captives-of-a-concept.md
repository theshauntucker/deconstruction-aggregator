In *Captives of a Concept*, Don Cameron meticulously deconstructs one of the JW organization's most critical foundational claims: that in 1919, Jesus Christ appointed the Watchtower organization as his sole representative on earth. This claim undergirds all organizational authority and doctrinal pronouncements. Cameron examines the historical and scriptural basis for this assertion and demonstrates it is entirely fabricated.

Understanding this single claim is essential for grasping how JW institutional authority functions. Once this foundational claim fails, the entire hierarchical structure that justifies Watchtower control becomes illegitimate. Cameron's systematic analysis is invaluable for members questioning organizational legitimacy.

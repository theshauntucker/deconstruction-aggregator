# Jehovah's Witnesses

Jehovah's Witnesses is a Christian offshoot movement founded in the 1870s by Charles Taze Russell in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. The organization claims approximately 8.7 million active publishers worldwide and operates under the watch of the Watchtower Bible and Tract Society. The religion is characterized by strict doctrinal control, disfellowshipping/shunning practices, and a hierarchical governance structure centered on the "Governing Body."

Critical concerns include: forced shunning of family members and friends who leave or dissent; the blanket refusal of blood transfusions even in life-threatening medical emergencies; systematic cover-ups of child sexual abuse cases; widespread institutional failures to report abuse to authorities; failed prophetic predictions (particularly the "1975" prediction); the two-witness rule that protects abusers; and pervasive information control through limiting exposure to outside sources. Extensive documentation from survivors, journalists, and government investigations reveals patterns of institutional protection of abusers and psychological harm inflicted through enforced isolation.

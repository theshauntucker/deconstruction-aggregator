The Australian Royal Commission's Case Study 29 represents one of the most comprehensive government investigations into Jehovah's Witnesses' handling of child sexual abuse. The inquiry documented over 1,000 alleged perpetrators within Australian JW congregations and found that the Watchtower organization systematically failed to report any cases to law enforcement.

The report exposed the mechanics of institutional failure: reliance on the two-witness rule (which requires two eyewitnesses to sexual abuse—an impossibility in most cases), internal judicial committees handling abuse allegations in secret, and deliberate policies that prioritized organizational reputation over victim protection. This authoritative government document provides irrefutable evidence that the abuse crisis was not incidental but structural to JW institutional practices.

Abby Stein's "Becoming Eve" is a memoir of leaving Hasidic Judaism while navigating gender identity, documenting her journey from a privileged position as a descendant of the Baal Shem Tov (founder of Hasidic Judaism) to her transition as a transgender woman and eventual departure from the community. The book explores the intersection of gender dysphoria and religious doubt, showing how theological rigidity about gender amplified questions about the entire religious system. Stein's work is particularly important for understanding LGBTQ experiences within ultra-Orthodox Judaism.

The memoir is valuable for understanding how ultra-Orthodox communities' strict gender roles not only oppress those assigned female at birth but also invalidate transgender and non-binary identities entirely. Stein's story demonstrates that questioning one aspect of religious ideology can ripple into wholesale religious deconstruction.

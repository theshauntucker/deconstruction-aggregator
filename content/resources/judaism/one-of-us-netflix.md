"One of Us" is a Netflix documentary following three individuals in their journeys leaving ultra-Orthodox Hasidic communities in Brooklyn. The film provides intimate access to the challenges of religious transition: the grief of family estrangement, the practical difficulties of employment with limited secular education, the psychological impact of deconstruction, and the courage required to build new lives outside insular communities. The documentary balances individual stories with systemic critique, showing both personal struggles and community dynamics.

The accessible documentary format makes this an excellent entry point for those beginning to understand OTD experiences or for family members and friends of OTD individuals seeking to understand the challenges their loved ones face. The multiple perspectives provide diverse views of what leaving ultra-Orthodox Judaism looks like.

The Netflix miniseries "Unorthodox" adapts Deborah Feldman's memoir into a four-episode drama exploring the experience of leaving a Satmar Hasidic community. The series dramatizes the emotional and psychological dimensions of religious transition, showing both the tight-knit community life OTD individuals leave behind and the exhilarating and terrifying prospect of building a new life outside. The visual storytelling makes the experience viscerally understandable for viewers unfamiliar with ultra-Orthodox life.

While the series takes creative liberties with the memoir, it captures essential truths about what leaving insular Orthodox communities means: the loss of family, the discovery of freedom, the grief and confusion that accompany deconstruction, and the resilience required to rebuild identity outside the only community one has known. The series is accessible to wide audiences while remaining authentic to OTD experiences.

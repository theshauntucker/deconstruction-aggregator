"Off the Derech" (OTD) refers to individuals who have left Orthodox, Haredi, or Hasidic Judaism—communities characterized by strict observance of Jewish law, separation from secular society, and hierarchical rabbinic authority. While Judaism itself is diverse and many Jews practice their faith with flexibility and secular integration, ultra-Orthodox communities maintain high walls between themselves and the outside world, creating unique challenges for those who question or leave the faith.

Those raised in insular Orthodox communities often face profound obstacles when leaving: limited secular education impairs employment prospects, arranged marriages may trap people in unwanted relationships, and family shunning can result in complete social and economic abandonment. Additionally, some ultra-Orthodox communities have faced scandals involving child sexual abuse with rabbinical cover-ups rather than reporting to authorities. This section provides resources for understanding Orthodox Jewish theology and practice, supporting OTD individuals through their transition, and examining the systemic issues within ultra-Orthodox communities.

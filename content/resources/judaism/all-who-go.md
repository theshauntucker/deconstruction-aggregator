Shulem Deen's "All Who Go Do Not Return" is a haunting memoir of leaving the Skverer Hasidic community and the devastating personal cost of religious transition. Deen describes his journey from devout Hasidic life to questions about faith, his eventual departure, and the subsequent loss of custody of his children—a tragedy common to OTD individuals whose religious departure is used against them in custody disputes. The memoir explores the theological and philosophical dimensions of leaving while centering the real human cost of such transitions.

The book is particularly important for understanding the stakes of leaving ultra-Orthodox Judaism, especially for fathers who may lose access to their children. Deen's eloquent narrative captures both the intellectual journey away from faith and the grief of estrangement from loved ones, making it essential reading for those understanding the emotional complexity of religious transition.

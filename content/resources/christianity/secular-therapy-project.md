The Secular Therapy Project, run by Recovering from Religion, addresses a critical practical problem faced by people deconstructing from religion: finding mental health treatment that doesn't inadvertently reinforce the religious trauma they're trying to address. In many parts of the country, particularly in the Bible Belt and other regions with high religious populations, default assumptions in therapy can favor faith-based counseling and may pathologize legitimate questions about religion.

The directory provides a searchable database of therapists who explicitly identify as secular or non-religious and who have committed to not imposing religious frameworks on their clients. This is particularly crucial for people recovering from religious trauma, purity culture damage, fundamentalist abuse, or authoritarian religious structures—clients need therapists who understand that leaving religion is often the healthiest choice, not a problem to be corrected. The Project vets therapists to ensure they understand religious trauma and deconstruction as legitimate clinical concerns rather than spiritual crises.

For someone in a conservative area seeking affirming mental health support during deconstruction, this directory is often the difference between finding genuinely helpful therapy and encountering providers who inadvertently undermine recovery.

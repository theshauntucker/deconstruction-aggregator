"Straight White American Jesus" is an academic podcast examining how white evangelicalism became fused with American nationalism, right-wing politics, and white supremacist ideology. Hosted by religion scholars including Kristin Kobes Du Mez (author of "Jesus and John Wayne"), the podcast analyzes the historical and theological roots of Christian nationalism, documents how evangelical institutions perpetuate white supremacy, and explains the connection between fundamentalist theology and political authoritarianism.

The podcast is particularly valuable for understanding deconstruction in its political dimensions—many evangelicals undergo faith deconstruction when they recognize the explicit connection between their religious community's theology and support for policies that harm vulnerable populations. Episodes examine how Christian teachings have been weaponized to support racial hierarchies, patriarchal structures, and authoritarian leaders. The academic rigor and historical documentation provide intellectual scaffolding for understanding why one's religious community's political positions might warrant fundamental reconsideration of the theology underlying them.

For evangelicals recognizing the Christian nationalist connections in their communities and traditions, "Straight White American Jesus" provides crucial context for understanding that this is not a recent aberration but a long historical pattern—making deconstruction feel less like personal failure and more like appropriate response to documented institutional harm.

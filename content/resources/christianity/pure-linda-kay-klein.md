"Pure" by Linda Kay Klein is the definitive investigation into evangelical purity culture and the profound psychological damage it causes, particularly to women and girls. Klein spent 15 years interviewing over 100 women raised in purity-focused evangelical environments, documenting how abstinence-only teachings, shame-based sexual education, and the policing of female bodies created lasting trauma including sexual dysfunction, anxiety disorders, obsessive-compulsive symptoms, dissociation, and disrupted relationships with sexuality and embodiment.

Klein traces purity culture's history within evangelicalism (particularly its acceleration in the 1990s and 2000s) and documents how the movement teaches young women to internalize shame about their bodies and sexuality while placing responsibility for men's sexual thoughts onto women's modesty. The book includes Klein's own story of struggling with OCD and sexual shame, alongside dozens of other women's experiences of healing and deconstruction. Klein also addresses the intersectional impacts on LGBTQ individuals and the role of purity culture in trauma bonding and abusive relationships.

For anyone raised in evangelical or conservative Christian environments where purity culture was emphasized—particularly millennial women who grew up during the peak of the purity movement—"Pure" provides both validation of their experience and a clinical understanding of how a well-intentioned theology created measurable psychological harm. The book is often the first comprehensive resource that helps survivors understand their symptoms as resulting from cultural indoctrination rather than personal moral failure.

Journey Free was founded by Dr. Marlene Winell, a clinical psychologist who coined and defined the term "Religious Trauma Syndrome" (RTS) to describe the documented psychological effects of authoritarian religious indoctrination, particularly fundamentalism. Winell's work established that religious trauma is a legitimate clinical phenomenon with identifiable symptoms including cognitive distortions, emotional dysregulation, dissociation, and disrupted attachment patterns—not simply a crisis of faith but a psychological injury requiring specific treatment approaches.

The organization provides online support groups led by trained facilitators, intensive retreat experiences for deeper processing of religious trauma, individual and family counseling referrals, and educational resources for both survivors and mental health professionals. Winell's approach is unique in the field for treating religious trauma not as a spiritual problem but as a psychological one, validating the very real neurobiological and emotional impacts of religious indoctrination and control.

For someone experiencing the aftermath of fundamentalist or authoritarian Christian upbringing, Winell's framework provides crucial validation that their struggles are real, documented, and treatable—and that leaving religion is often the necessary first step toward healing, not a symptom of a spiritual crisis.

## Christianity and Religious Deconstruction

Christianity is the world's largest religion with approximately 2.4 billion adherents, spanning numerous denominations, theological traditions, and cultural expressions. This makes it also the dominant religion undergoing deconstruction in the Western world. The Christian deconstruction space is the broadest and most active of any religious tradition, with extensive peer support networks, published memoirs, documentary films, podcasts, and academic resources dedicated to the experiences of those questioning and leaving the faith.

The resources in this section address the major theological and cultural issues driving Christian deconstruction: the doctrine of biblical inerrancy and the documented contradictions within scripture, the doctrine of eternal punishment and hell, the purity culture movement and its profound psychological harm (particularly to women and LGBTQ individuals), systemic clergy sexual abuse and institutional cover-ups (especially in the Catholic Church), prosperity gospel theology, young earth creationism and science denial, the exclusion and condemnation of LGBTQ individuals, complementarian gender hierarchies, and the findings of historical and critical biblical scholarship that undermine fundamentalist biblical claims.

## Major Branches and Issues

### Evangelical Protestantism
Evangelical Christianity encompasses a diverse spectrum of independent churches, megachurches, and loosely affiliated movements emphasizing biblical authority, personal conversion ("born again" experience), and active proselytization. Key deconstruction issues include the assumed inerrancy of the Bible, end-times theology and apocalyptic thinking, the "prosperity gospel" and health-wealth theology, purity culture in youth ministries, authoritarian church leadership structures, and Christian nationalism. The #exvangelical hashtag and movement represent one of the most visible faith deconstruction movements in contemporary culture.

### Catholicism
The Roman Catholic Church, with over 1 billion adherents, faces sustained scrutiny regarding clergy sexual abuse scandals, institutional cover-ups spanning decades, the role of celibacy in perpetuating abuse, authoritarian hierarchical structures, reproductive oppression through teachings on contraception and abortion, and the historical suppression of critical biblical scholarship. Catholic deconstruction often involves reconciling childhood faith formation with historical awareness of institutional corruption and doctrinal problems.

### Purity Culture
Purity culture—the evangelical and fundamentalist Christian movement emphasizing virginity, sexual shame, and abstinence-only ideology—has created documented psychological harm including sexual dysfunction, shame-based anxiety disorders, and trauma. This movement particularly affected young people from the 1990s onward and has become a major focal point for deconstruction narratives, especially among millennial women.

## The Deconstruction Landscape

The Christian deconstruction community benefits from a mature infrastructure of peer support (Recovering from Religion, The Clergy Project), clinical expertise (Dr. Marlene Winell's work on Religious Trauma Syndrome), prominent defectors and public deconstruction narratives (Josh Harris, Bart Ehrman, Dan Barker), and extensive documentary and media examinations of Christian harm. This rich ecosystem of resources makes Christianity deconstruction uniquely supported compared to other religions.

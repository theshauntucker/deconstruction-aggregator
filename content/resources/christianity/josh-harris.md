Josh Harris, author of the 1997 bestseller "I Kissed Dating Goodbye" that became the foundational text of evangelical purity culture, publicly deconstructed from evangelical Christianity in 2019—a watershed moment for the exvangelical movement. Harris' book had influenced millions of evangelical young people, particularly girls and women, promoting courtship-only dating models and shame-based approaches to sexuality. His public apology for the harm his book caused and his subsequent deconstruction narratives represent both personal transformation and cultural accountability.

Harris has shared detailed accounts of his reasons for leaving Christianity, his re-examination of the theological claims he once promoted, and his recognition of the damage purity culture caused to millions of young people. His public deconstruction was significant not just as a personal journey but as a cultural moment—the author of one of evangelicalism's most influential purity culture texts explicitly recognizing and apologizing for the harm it caused. For people who grew up reading "I Kissed Dating Goodbye," Harris' deconstruction validates their own experiences of shame and harm caused by purity teachings.

Josh Harris's trajectory from prominent evangelical to public deconstructer demonstrates that deconstruction is not an intellectual or spiritual failure but sometimes a process of honest reckoning with how one has contributed to harm and choosing a different path forward.

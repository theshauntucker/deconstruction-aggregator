The Clergy Project is a uniquely important organization serving a specific population: current and former religious leaders—pastors, priests, ministers, rabbis, imams, and other clergy—who have lost faith in the supernatural claims of their religions but face extraordinary barriers to leaving (financial dependence on the clergy position, family pressure, community status, uncertainty about career transition, public visibility). The organization maintains strict confidentiality to protect members' identities and livelihoods, creating a safe space for religious leaders to process doubts and plan transitions without immediate exposure.

With over 1,000 members, the Project provides peer support from others who understand the unique challenges of clergy deconstruction: the cognitive dissonance of performing faith while privately disbelieving, the complexity of family and community identity, the practical logistics of career transition and financial independence, and the grief of losing a sense of purpose and community status. The organization also provides resources on how to transition out of clergy positions safely, alternative career paths, and managing the identity shift from religious leader to secular person.

For Christian clergy members questioning their faith—a phenomenon documented across denominations—The Clergy Project represents both the possibility of leaving and a community of people who have successfully navigated the professional and personal complexities of exiting the clergy.

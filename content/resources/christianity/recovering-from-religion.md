Recovering from Religion is the closest existing thing to a cross-religion support infrastructure for people leaving faith. As a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, they operate the national support hotline 1-844-I-DOUBT-IT (1-844-438-2848), which connects callers with trained volunteers who have themselves gone through religious deconstruction. The organization provides completely confidential, non-judgmental peer support for anyone questioning or leaving religion of any faith tradition, making it an essential first resource for someone in crisis during active deconstruction.

Beyond the crisis hotline, Recovering from Religion facilitates online support groups organized by religion and deconstruction stage, provides a directory of secular therapists trained to understand religious trauma, and maintains resources specifically addressing the harms of purity culture, fundamentalism, and authoritarian religious structures. The organization's model of peer support—where volunteers who have lived through deconstruction support those currently experiencing it—creates a powerful sense of recognition and non-pathologization of the deconstruction experience.

The organization's cross-religious approach means that whether someone is leaving evangelicalism, Catholicism, conservative Christianity, or another faith tradition entirely, they can connect with peers who understand the specific experience of faith deconstruction and transition to a secular life.

The Liturgists Podcast, founded by musician Michael Gungor and host Science Mike, provides a gentler on-ramp into faith questioning for people not yet ready for full deconversion narratives. The podcast bridges faith and science, explores progressive theology and biblical scholarship, and creates space for people to question religious claims without requiring immediate rejection of religious identity. The tone is thoughtful, non-judgmental, and explicitly designed for people in transition—those still exploring their relationship with faith rather than already committed to leaving.

Science Mike (Mike McHargue) approaches faith questions from a perspective of genuine intellectual curiosity rather than apologetic defense or aggressive criticism. The podcast covers topics including biblical interpretation, the relationship between faith and science, sexuality and gender in religious contexts, and the human meaning-making functions of religion. The approach assumes that someone can simultaneously acknowledge scientific evidence that contradicts traditional religious claims while still finding meaning in religious practice and community.

For evangelical listeners particularly, The Liturgists offers a middle path that many people occupy during deconstruction: maintaining spiritual identity and practice while adopting a more critical, evidence-based approach to theological claims. For some, this remains their permanent position; for others, it's a transition point toward full secularization. The podcast validates both journeys.

"Shiny Happy People," a 2023 Amazon Prime documentary, exposes the Institute in Basic Life Principles (IBLP) founded by Bill Gothard and the Duggar family's connection to Gothard's teachings. The documentary reveals the systematic physical abuse, sexual abuse cover-ups, and authoritarian control structures embedded within IBLP-affiliated fundamentalist homeschooling communities. Through interviews with survivors and whistleblowers, the film documents how IBLP's teachings on parental authority, female submission, and corporal punishment created environments where abuse thrived and was actively concealed.

The documentary is particularly significant because it makes visible the mechanisms of abuse within fundamentalist Christianity—not as individual failures or isolated incidents, but as systemic consequences of authoritarian theologies that prioritize parental and patriarchal control over child safety. The film documents how IBLP communities actively worked to prevent reporting of abuse and pressure victims into silence, using scripture to justify physical punishment and sexual coercion. The Duggar family's public platform and connection to IBLP provided a case study in how fundamentalist abuse operates in plain sight within evangelical culture.

For anyone raised in fundamentalist Christian homeschooling communities, IBLP-affiliated environments, or similar high-control systems, "Shiny Happy People" provides both validation and explanation of why their childhood experiences involved abuse that was normalized within their religious community. The documentary is an important cultural artifact of accountability and awareness in Christian deconstruction.

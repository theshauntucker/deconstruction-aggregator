"The Keepers" is a 2017 Netflix documentary investigating the unsolved 1969 murder of Sister Cathy Cesnik, a nun who taught at a Baltimore Catholic high school, and the context of systematic clergy sexual abuse within the Catholic Church. The documentary reveals how the Catholic institutional hierarchy systematically covered up abuse, protected abusers, pressured victims into silence, and actively hindered investigation. The film documents not just individual abuse but institutional corruption designed to preserve the church's public image at the cost of survivors' justice and safety.

The documentary is significant because it makes visible the mechanisms of institutional abuse cover-up within Catholicism—not as isolated incidents but as systematic patterns supported by the church hierarchy. Victims explain how they were ignored, blamed, or pressured to forgive their abusers; the film documents how the church moved abusive priests to new parishes where they continued abusing without consequence. For Catholics who believed their institution was fundamentally good despite known abuse problems, "The Keepers" provides documentation of institutional corruption that undermines apologetics about the church's moral authority.

For anyone raised Catholic or with Catholic family members, "The Keepers" documents why the abuse crisis is not a problem incidental to Catholicism but central to how the institution actually operates—a recognition that often catalyzes Catholic deconstruction.

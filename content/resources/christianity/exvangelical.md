The Exvangelical Podcast, hosted by Blake Chastain who coined the #exvangelical hashtag that named an entire movement, provides detailed exploration of evangelical deconstruction at both personal and cultural levels. Blake interviews prominent figures in the exvangelical movement, shares stories from listeners in various stages of deconstruction, and examines the historical and cultural factors that shaped evangelicalism and are now driving its dissolution among younger generations.

The podcast documents what "exvangelical" means—not simply leaving evangelicalism for another Christian denomination, but a complete departure from evangelical belief and practice combined with critical examination of evangelical culture's role in American politics, abuse patterns, and social harm. Episodes cover topics including the connection between evangelicalism and Christian nationalism, the impact of purity culture, clergy sexual abuse, the prosperity gospel, and the public deconstruction narratives of prominent evangelicals. The podcast serves as both witness and archive for a cultural moment where evangelical influence in America is visibly declining.

For anyone who grew up evangelical, the Exvangelical Podcast provides both community and context—evidence that their deconstruction is part of a larger cultural shift, not an isolated personal failing, and that leaving evangelicalism is not just a faith question but often a political and ethical awakening.

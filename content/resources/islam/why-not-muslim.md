"Why I Am Not a Muslim" by Ibn Warraq (pseudonym of a scholar who remains anonymous for safety reasons) is a foundational critical work examining Islamic theology, history, and practice. The book offers rigorous scholarly analysis of Quranic contradictions, hadith reliability, Muhammad's life and teachings, Islamic law, and the historical accuracy of Islamic sources. Warraq draws on decades of Islamic scholarship to present a comprehensive case for the rational critique of Islamic doctrine.

Published in 1995 and regularly updated, the book serves as both intellectual ammunition for those deconstructing Islam and a scholarly resource for understanding the strongest secular critiques of Islamic faith. Warraq's work is particularly valuable for advanced readers seeking deep engagement with theological and historical arguments, and his use of pseudonym reflects the genuine risks many ex-Muslim scholars and writers face.

The Council of Ex-Muslims of Britain publishes comprehensive reports documenting apostasy laws and their application in countries around the world. These reports detail the legal penalties for leaving Islam, case studies of individuals prosecuted or harmed under apostasy laws, and the human rights implications of religious thought-control. The research combines legal analysis with personal testimony, illustrating the gap between theory and practice in how apostasy laws affect real people.

For those interested in the global context of religious freedom and apostasy rights, these reports provide rigorous documentation that complements individual stories with systemic analysis. The reports serve as advocacy tools for international human rights organizations while also providing validation and context for ex-Muslims experiencing persecution under apostasy laws.

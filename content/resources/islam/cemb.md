The Council of Ex-Muslims of Britain (CEMB) is a leading organization advocating for the rights of ex-Muslims in the UK and internationally. Founded to challenge the stigmatization of apostasy and promote secular values within Muslim-majority contexts, CEMB operates openly in defiance of apostasy taboos, serving as a visible beacon for those questioning Islam in restrictive environments. The organization provides support, advocacy, and resources for individuals at various stages of religious transition.

CEMB is particularly known for documenting apostasy laws globally and publishing reports on the human rights violations faced by those who leave Islam. The organization combines direct support for affected individuals with public advocacy and research, making it a comprehensive resource for understanding both the practical and political dimensions of leaving Islam.

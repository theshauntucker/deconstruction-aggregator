Ayaan Hirsi Ali's "Heretic" argues for a reformation of Islam similar to the Christian Reformation, proposing structural changes to Islamic theology and practice to align with contemporary secular values. Rather than calling for abandonment of Islam entirely, Hirsi Ali advocates for Muslims who prioritize human rights, gender equality, and freedom of conscience to reform Islamic doctrine from within. The book offers practical critique of Islamic practices while engaging seriously with the possibility of reformed Islam.

For readers interested in both secular critique and the possibility of religious reform, "Heretic" provides an intermediate position between acceptance of traditional Islam and complete rejection of the faith. The work acknowledges the diversity within Islam and the potential for change while remaining unflinching about the current problems within Islamic orthodoxy.

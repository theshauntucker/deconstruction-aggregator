Islam has approximately 1.9 billion adherents worldwide, making it the second-largest religion by population. The faith encompasses diverse theological traditions (Sunni, Shia, Sufi) and practices across different cultures and regions. However, critical examination of Islamic texts, history, and modern practice raises significant concerns, particularly around apostasy laws (which carry capital punishment in 12 countries), women's rights restrictions, LGBTQ persecution, and the treatment of minorities.

For individuals raised in Islamic cultures seeking to deconstruct their faith or leave the religion entirely—a journey often called "leaving Islam"—the path can be psychologically complex and physically dangerous. Many ex-Muslims face family rejection, social ostracization, and in some contexts, threats to their safety. This section curates resources from scholars, organizations, and individuals who critically examine Islamic theology and provide support for ex-Muslims navigating their transition.

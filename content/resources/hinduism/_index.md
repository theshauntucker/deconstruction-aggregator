Hinduism encompasses the spiritual and cultural traditions of over 1.2 billion adherents, making it one of the world's oldest continuously practiced religions. While often presented as philosophically sophisticated and spiritually advanced, institutional Hinduism has perpetuated some of the world's most entrenched systems of oppression. The caste system has structured Indian society for millennia, creating hierarchies of ritual purity that determine access to education, economic opportunity, marriage partners, and basic dignity for those designated as ritually polluted.

Beyond caste-based oppression, contemporary Hinduism faces significant deconversion issues including guru abuse across multiple ashrams and institutions, the rise of Hindu nationalism (Hindutva) as a political ideology, persistent superstitious practices that harm vulnerable populations, systematic gender discrimination, and the normalization of child marriage in some communities. Gurus operating ashrams and spiritual organizations have perpetrated sexual abuse, financial exploitation, and emotional manipulation, often justified through appeals to spiritual development and karma.

This section provides resources for those deconstructing from Hinduism, exploring the faith's spiritual claims while critically examining its institutional harms and systemic oppression. Materials address both the philosophical deconstruction of Hindu teachings and the practical support needed by those leaving communities built on Hindu theology and practice.

# Troublemaker: Surviving Hollywood and Scientology

Leah Remini's memoir provides an intimate account of life as a celebrity within Scientology. Remini joined the church as a child and remained for 34 years before leaving in 2013. Her book details the day-to-day reality of high-control group membership, the privilege and vulnerability of celebrity status within the organization, and the emotional complexity of disconnection.

*Troublemaker* is highly accessible and personal, making it an excellent entry point for understanding Scientology's impact on individuals. Remini's subsequent activism through *Scientology and the Aftermath* and the Fair Game Podcast has made her one of the most visible and effective public critics of the organization.

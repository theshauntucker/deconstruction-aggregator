# Scientology

Scientology was founded in 1954 by L. Ron Hubbard and claims between 20,000 and 50,000 active members worldwide. The organization is widely considered one of the most litigious religious groups, and investigation requires extra legal care. Key issues include the disconnection policy (forced separation from family members who leave or critique), the Rehabilitation Project Force (RPF—isolation and labor for those deemed non-compliant), aggressive financial exploitation through the "Bridge to Total Freedom," and the controversial Fair Game doctrine authorizing harassment of critics.

The organization's extreme secrecy around upper-level teachings (OT materials), combined with accounts from hundreds of former members and extensive court documentation, provides substantial material for critical analysis. This section aggregates the most reliable resources for understanding Scientology's practices, history, and documented harms.
